  • Final proposal

You will get the most of out this class if you:

  1. engage with the readings and materials,
  2. use R to complete any analysis,
  3. ask for help immediately after giving it (whatever it is) a good faith effort, and
  4. keep an open and honest line of communication with me and your peers if possible.

Each type of assignment in this class helps with at least one one of these criteria.

Final proposal

Throughout the course, you will have weekly opportunities to expand and refine your skill set culminating in the development of an instrument which will serve as a final project1.

Week 1: Think about assessment.

Construct a one paragraph case for a population2 that needs to be researched, why it should be be studied, and how the data would be quantitative.


Week 2: Create an argument.

Submit a one to three page proposal outlining specific questions that you would like to assess and then describe an instrument that would be used to assess those items. Include general details on what measures would be used.


Week 3. Develop a draft.

Create a draft of your instrument3 by whatever medium you choose and then derive a skeleton. Map the measures directly to the items and group them if possible.


Week 4. Refine the draft.

Based on feedback, amend the instrument if necessary and resubmit for further assessment. Explicitly detail what changes were made using a matrix. After approval, administer the instrument for pilot testing.


Week 5. Address a priori and post analyses.

Submit a one to three page with a description of what approaches were used to assess validity and reliability, and all corresponding data and analyses by csv and an R script, respectively.


Week 6. Refine and submit final instrument.

After amending, submit the final version of your instrument.

  1. These will not be graded using a check system. If you’ve engaged with the course content and completed the exercises and mini projects throughout the course, you should do just fine with the final project.↩︎

  2. A population does not necessarily refer to people.↩︎

  3. If the instrument is housed elsewhere (e.g. Qualtrics), then simply provide a link and ensure that access is granted to me.↩︎
