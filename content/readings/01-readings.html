Read Things

Data visualization isn’t just about making pretty pictures1, rather it is done to convey a great deal of information in an efficient way. With that said, you have probably come across visualizations in the media, online, etc that simply don’t make sense or are confusing. Can you recall one that was just terrible? Think about that question as you go through this week’s readings:

  1. WAINER, H. (1984). How to Display Data Badly. The American Statistician, 38(2), 137-147.
  2. Tufte, E. R. (2001). Graphical Excellence. The visual display of quantitative information (2nd ed., pp. 13-51). Graphics Press.
  3. Tufte, E. R. (2001). Graphical Integrity. The visual display of quantitative information (2nd ed., pp. 52-77). Graphics Press.
