With that said, I am not concerned whether you know a formula or not, rather I want you to understand the general idea so please take your time on Chapter 5!

Suggestions

Take some time to review or get a first look at some basic statistics. Again, I do not care if you can memorize a formula. We have these things called books and the Internet to get those. Rather if you can understand what’s going on and when to use a method (e.g. when is it more appropriate to take the mean, median, or mode of a sample).

  • The statistics videos over at Crash Course are fantastic and pretty entertaining. If interested, you can see the entire list of Crash Course: Statistics series by clicking here. The example video below is on sampling and bias.

  • A good resource for doing foundational level statistics in R, check out Learning statistics with R: A tutorial for psychology students and other beginners which is part of an ongoing effort to bring statistics with R to the masses.

Questions to reflect on1

  • What do descriptive statistics really tell us?
  • What can’t descriptive statistics tell us?
  • How are both assessment and evaluation used in CTT (classical test theory)?
  • Why is reliability important?
  • How is reliability used in analyzing qualitative data?
